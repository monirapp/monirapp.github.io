Let's talk about collections a little bit

 

I put undated collections from back to front — this allows me to quickly reference them when needed.

 

 

One little tip when starting out:

Don't set up too many pages and collections at first. You will probably change your system and discover what works for you, or doesn't. Add a collection when you need it, that way you know you won't add pages you'll never use.

Make the system your own

I'm a firm believer of using the right tool for the right task. And I don't believe the Bullet Journal is the best tool for everything.

 

Combining digital and analog

I still prefer my endless master task list in Wunderlist (categorized per project and using hashtags). I still prefer to add my “To Read” books on Goodreads because that too is endless. This all means that I can rather quickly move to a new notebook when I need to, because I only keep the things that are relevant to me right now in my current notebook. This is also why I might digitize the “Bookcase unread” list, because I’m taking too long to get through it (at least 3 notebooks now), so I can better have it my Wunderlist or Evernote and track my progress in there.

I also archive old or irrelevant collections in Evernote, simply by using the scan feature in the Evernote app. This way, I can easily reference them when needed, at any point in the future and at any location.

 

 

 

Another way I combine digital and analog is time tracking. While I definitely track my tasks and activities in my Bullet Journal, I don't track the time my work related tasks take. It's just too much hassle for me and I need reminders and automatic time tracking. In my case, I use Toggle on desktop, with a regular reminder to start the timer because otherwise I'll forget…

Let your journal change with you

My life changes, as does your probably. Sometimes I don't need a weekly setup, or even a monthly one. Sometimes I skip weeks of writing. It's alright! Don't stress about forgetting about your journal or keeping pages open for the missed days.
